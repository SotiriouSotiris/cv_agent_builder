# CV Interview Question Bank

Use these prompts selectively. Start broad, then choose follow-ups that respond to the candidate's answers. Do not read the list to the candidate as a single questionnaire.

## Initial material and preferences

- Do you have any old CVs, profiles, portfolios, repositories, certificates, publications, or other resources I should inspect first?
- What kinds of work would you like a future CV to help you pursue?
- Which kinds of work are you strongest at, even if you are not targeting a specific title?
- Where are you applying, and are you considering remote or relocation options?
- Is there anything you do not want disclosed or emphasised?

## Role chronology

- Walk me through how you joined this organisation and how your role changed.
- What was your official title, and were there responsibilities beyond that title?
- What did the product or team build, and who used it?
- Who else was on the team, and what did you personally own?
- Which dates are exact and which are approximate?

## Achievement probes

- What is the hardest problem you solved there?
- Which system or feature did you design from the beginning?
- What decision would not have happened without your input?
- What constraints made the work difficult: time, accuracy, scale, regulation, reliability, or legacy code?
- What broke, changed, improved, launched, or became possible because of the work?
- How did you know the solution worked?
- Is there a number, range, or before-and-after comparison you can share?
- If numbers are confidential, what qualitative impact can be stated safely?

## Technical depth

- What was the full stack, and which parts did you work on directly?
- How was data modelled and protected?
- What integrations, APIs, payments, authentication, or background processing were involved?
- What testing, CI/CD, monitoring, and incident-response practices did you own?
- What architectural trade-offs did you make?
- What performance, security, data-integrity, or reliability work did you do?
- Did you build a prototype, production system, migration, or ongoing platform?

## Leadership without title inflation

- Did you plan work, review code, mentor people, coordinate releases, or communicate with stakeholders?
- Which of those duties were formally assigned, and which emerged informally?
- Who made final product and staffing decisions?
- How large was the team?
- Did you help someone onboard, grow, or deliver more effectively?
- What should the CV say so it captures the leadership without changing your official title?

## Founder and independent-product work

- What problem does the product solve and for whom?
- When did you begin, prototype, launch, and reach each platform?
- Who are the founders, and how are responsibilities divided?
- What did you personally build and continue to maintain?
- What production, support, compliance, billing, analytics, and operational work is involved?
- What adoption or commercial evidence can be shared?
- Which advertised features are live, planned, or experimental?
- What was the most difficult engineering or business constraint?

## Projects and publications

- Which project best demonstrates a skill not visible in your employment history?
- Was it completed, published, used, or maintained?
- What did you build rather than follow from a tutorial?
- Is there a public link, repository, package, extension, paper, or demo?
- Does the project strengthen the target CV enough to justify its space?

## Education and early experience

- Which qualification, dissertation, award, teaching, service, or volunteering experience remains relevant?
- Did it produce a useful project, publication, leadership example, or unusually strong result?
- Should early material be retained for context or removed to prioritise recent work?

## Skills reconciliation

- Which technologies would you confidently use in a professional role today?
- Which have you used in production rather than only learning?
- Which skills are missing from the preliminary list?
- Which listed skills are outdated or no longer representative?
- Which spoken languages should appear, and what proficiency level can be claimed accurately?

## Final verification

- Which claims or dates are approximate?
- Which achievements were team efforts, and how should your contribution be described?
- Are any metrics confidential or likely to become outdated?
- Is any planned work currently described as if it were live?
- Are your contact details and public links current?
- Is there anything here that a former manager or collaborator would describe differently?
